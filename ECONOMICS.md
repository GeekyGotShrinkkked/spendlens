# Economics

## What a Converted Lead is Worth to Credex

A converted lead = a startup that books a consultation
and purchases discounted AI credits through Credex.

**Estimating deal size:**
- Average startup paying $500-2000/month on AI tools
- Credex discount is 25-35% below retail
- A startup spending $1000/month saves $300/month
  by switching to Credex credits
- They buy in bulk — typical purchase is 3-6 months
  of credits upfront
- Average deal size estimate: $2000-5000 per customer

**Credex margin estimate:**
- Credex sources credits from companies that
  overforecast — buying at steep discount
- Estimated margin: 15-25% on each deal
- Revenue per converted customer: $300-1250

**Conservative estimate: $500 revenue per
converted customer (lifetime, first purchase)**

## What Makes This Profitable

SpendLens is already profitable at $0 CAC.
The question is whether it scales.

For the tool to drive meaningful revenue:
- Need consistent traffic of 1000+ visitors per week
- Need audit completion rate above 50%
- Need email capture rate above 30%
- Need consultation booking rate above 15%
  for high savings leads

All four are achievable with the GTM plan.
The Hacker News launch alone can drive 1000+
visitors in a single day.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

**Month 1-3: Validation**
- Launch on Hacker News and Reddit
- Target 500 audits per week by end of month 3
- Expected revenue: $8000/month
- Key metric: consultation booking rate

**Month 4-6: Optimization**
- A/B test results page to improve email capture
- Add benchmark mode to increase shareability
- Target 1500 audits per week
- Expected revenue: $24000/month

**Month 7-12: Scale**
- Add more tools to the audit engine
- Launch embeddable widget for finance blogs
- Partner with startup communities for distribution
- Target 5000 audits per week
- Expected revenue: $80000/month

**Month 13-18: $1M ARR**
- At $80000/month = $960000 ARR
- Gap to $1M ARR = $40000/month additional
- Close gap with direct outbound to high spend startups
  identified through audit data

**The math that has to be true:**
- 5000 audits per week = 260000 per year
- 40% email capture = 104000 leads per year
- 15% high savings = 15600 high value leads
- 20% consultation rate = 3120 consultations
- 60% close rate = 1872 customers
- $500 average revenue = $936000 ARR

Close enough to $1M ARR with conservative estimates.
The biggest variable is traffic — everything else
is conversion optimization.

## Biggest Risk

The audit engine needs to stay accurate as pricing
changes. If Cursor changes their pricing and we
don't update, we recommend wrong things and lose
trust instantly.

Mitigation: PRICING_DATA.md documents every source.
Set a monthly calendar reminder to verify all prices.
Long term: automate pricing checks via vendor APIs
or web scraping.
